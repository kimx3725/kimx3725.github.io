Dongmin (Dennis) Kim

dongmin_kim [at] harvard [dot] edu

I am a quantitative ecologist with a background in statistics and a founder/data curator of Move Disease Archive. I am interested in applying statistical and mathematical models to problems in ecology. My research interests are (1) animal movement models, (2) Hidden Markov models and their applications in disease ecology, (3) Habitat selection models, and (4) Spatial biodiversity modeling.

I am currently a postdoctoral associate co-advised by Jesús Pinto-Ledezma at UMN and Paul Moorcroft at Harvard University. I received my PhD at the University of Minnesota (UMN) in 2024, co-advised by Allison Shaw and John Fieberg. Before my PhD, I studied statistics at the University of Minnesota, Morris. Starting in September, I will join the Smithsonian Conservation Biology Institute's Conservation Ecology Center as a George E. Burch Postdoctoral Fellow.
