Bio

I am a quantitative ecologist with a background in statistics and a founder/data curator of Move Disease Archive. My research interests are pretty broad, but generally involve the application of statistical and mathematical models to problems in movement ecology.

I received my PhD at the University of Minnesota (UMN) in 2024, co-advised by Allison Shaw and John Fieberg. I am currently a postdoctoral associate co-advised by Jesús Pinto-Ledezma at UMN and Paul Moorcroft at Harvard University.

Throughout my career, I have worked with a dynamic group of researchers around the world, such as the Smithsonian Conservation Biology Institute and the Max Planck Institute of Animal Behavior. Collaboration is one of my favorite parts about being a researcher, so if you're interested in working together, please get in touch!

Interests

  • Animal movement models / Habitat selection models
    • Applications of step selection function (SSF).
    • Quantifying effects of hidden drivers in animal movement, such as memory.
  • Developing data infrastructure and methods in Disease / Movement ecology
    • Leading a global data hub (Move Disease Archive) for eco-epidemiological datasets integrating movement and diagnostic data.
    • Developing and applying hidden Markov models to detect disease progression in wildlife using animal movement data.
  • Migration ecology
    • Theoretical modeling of migratory strategies affected by pathogen infections.
    • Statistical analyses of variation in migratory strategies, especially partial migration.
  • Biodiversity models
    • Integrating biodiversity and environmental metadata.
    • Investigating how macroevolutionary processes and environmental conditions shape large-scale patterns of species diversity and co-occurrence.
